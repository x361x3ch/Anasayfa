Böhm-Bawerk asked a question Marx never answered. The output the worker produces is sold months or years later. The worker is paid today. Would the worker rather have $80 today or $100 a year from now? Almost always, $80 today. The gap between $80 and $100 is the price of time. This is time preference. The same person values the same good more today than tomorrow. Capitalists advance present goods in exchange for future goods, and bear the wait. That difference is interest. Marx had been calling it exploitation.

Then came the verdict from inside Marxism itself. Piero Sraffa, a Cambridge economist sympathetic to Marx, published "Production of Commodities by Means of Commodities" in 1960. He proved labor values are redundant by demonstrating that if you know the physical inputs of production and the real wage rate, you can mathematically calculate all relative prices and the rate of profit. You do not need to calculate "labor value" at any point. All prices and profit rates can be determined without it.
